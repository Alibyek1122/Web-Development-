Genghis Khan (c. 1162–1227), born Temüjin, was the founder and first Great Khan of the Mongol Empire, which became the largest contiguous land empire in history. After uniting nomadic tribes in the Mongolian steppe, he launched massive,often brutal campaigns across Asia, including China and Central Asia, while promoting religious tolerance and trade.
